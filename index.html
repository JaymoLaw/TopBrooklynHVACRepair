If you proceed with any contractor, request notes of the diagnostic steps performed, the test results (e.g., microfarads for capacitors, superheat/subcool numbers, combustion readings), and the exact parts replaced. Documentation makes warranties meaningful.

Due-Diligence Checklist (before you hire any HVAC contractor)

  • Request a written, itemized estimate and confirm whether the diagnostic fee is applied to repair costs.
  • Ask for brand, model, and part numbers before approving replacements; keep photos for your records.
  • Clarify warranty terms—labor vs. parts, and who handles manufacturer claims.
  • Confirm ETA windows in writing and what happens if the window is missed.
  • When possible, get a second opinion on system replacements.
